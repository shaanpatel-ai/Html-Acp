Honey bees

Honey bees are highly social, winged insects famous for their role in pollination and for producing honey and beeswax. Living in large, structured colonies, they operate with a strict division of labor between a single queen, worker bees, and drones. These essential pollinators help plants grow, playing a critical role in the ecosystem.

Honey bees fact

they build wax hives and communicate through complex "waggle dances". These insects are crucial for agriculture and ecosystem health, with a single worker producing a tiny amount of honey in her 5-6 week life.
