About pizza

Pizza is a savory Italian dish featuring a flattened, baked bread dough base, typically topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and various toppings. Originating in Naples as a working-class meal, it evolved into a globally popular food recognized for its distinctive,, often wood-fired crust. Key styles include Neapolitan (thin, soft), New York (large, foldable slice), and Chicago (deep-dish).

How to make a pizza

Making homemade pizza involves creating a simple yeast dough, letting it rise, topping it with sauce and mozzarella, and baking at high heat (\(500\)-\(550^{\circ}\text{F}\) / \(260\)-\(285^{\circ}\text{C}\)) for \(8\)-\(12\) minutes. Key techniques include using high-grade flour for the dough, stretching it by hand, and using a hot pizza stone or steel for a crispy crust
