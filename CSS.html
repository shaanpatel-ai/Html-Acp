India

History of India

The history of India, spanning over 5,000 years, began with the Indus Valley Civilization (c. 3300–1900 BCE). It evolved through the Vedic period, the rise of major empires like the Mauryas and Guptas, and medieval Sultanate/Mughal rule. After British colonial rule (1858–1947), India gained independence in 1947, becoming the world's largest democracy.

About India

India, officially the Republic of India (Bharat), is a vibrant South Asian nation, known as the world's largest democracy and 7th largest country by area. It boasts a massive $4.13 trillion economy (expected 2026) with significant growth in IT, space, and manufacturing. The country has a rich cultural history, with the Himalayas in the north, a long coastline, and diverse, populous cities.

Food of India

Indian food is a diverse tapestry of regional flavors, ranging from savory, wheat-based dishes in the north to rice-based dishes with coconut and spices in the south. Key highlights include rich curries, street food like samosas, and a vast array of vegetarian options like paneer and lentils, alongside savory snacks.
